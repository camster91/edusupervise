Three things I shipped this week for EduSupervise.

A teacher in Toronto sent me feedback last week. She runs her supervision schedule from a five-day rotation PDF she pulled from her school board's website. "I would have a little blurb saying that teachers can use this to start their own duty schedule calendar," she said.

So I built three things.

**1. A solo teacher path.** You don't need to be a school admin to use it. Any teacher can sign up, run the five-step wizard, and have their own duty rotation in under ten minutes. 205 teachers signed up in the first 30 days.

**2. Educational Assistants are a first-class user now.** They can be assigned to specific slots. They don't have to "complete" duties like teachers do — supervising is the whole job. Treated EAs as a flag on the teacher type for the first three months. Everyone was confused. Made them a separate role last week.

**3. PDF drop.** If your school already has a duty rotation in PDF form, drop it in. The system reads it and builds the week in under 500 milliseconds. No retyping, no AI vision hallucination, just pdfplumber table extraction.

If you're a teacher, an EA, or you know one — drop a comment with what eats the most time in your school's supervision rotation. I'll send you the rundown of how we built through the rough parts.

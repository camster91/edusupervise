## How to use this

The caption appears as overlay text on each App Store screenshot. Keep it short (5-8 words). 12th-grade English. No banned words (unlock, leverage, robust, synergy, holistic, dilute). One idea per sentence.

The 8 screenshots (3 per device class) tell a story in this order:
1. **Hero** — what the product does
2. **Differentiator** — why it's different (school-first, not generic)
3. **Flow** — the moment a user experiences value

## Universal "could be on any screenshot" callouts

If you have a 4th or 5th screenshot slot and need extras:

### Free / Pro framing (if you have plan tiers)
- Caption: **Free for schools under 10 teachers. Pro for everyone else.**
- Shows: Billing page
- Why: Honest pricing in the screenshot itself reduces the "is this enterprise?" doubt.

### Privacy / data residency
- Caption: **Your data stays in your school's region.**
- Shows: Settings > School settings
- Why: Schools care about FERPA / state laws. Naming "your region" reads as compliance.

### School-specific (camera-shot, not in-app)
- Caption: **Two schools. Five districts. 14,000 students scheduled.**
- Shows: Real photo of a teacher using the app on a school device
- Why: Real numbers beat claims. Adjust to your actual numbers.

## Writing rules (12th-grade)

- **DO**: short, specific, one number, the user-not-we voice
- **DON'T**: "we built", "leverage", "robust", "unlock your potential"
- **Length**: 5-8 words, max 10
- **Tone**: confident, plain, no exclamation marks
- **Test**: would a smart 18-year-old get every word?

## Sample caption rewrite (before → after)

Before: "Leverage our robust platform to unlock seamless duty scheduling!"
After: **Duty scheduling, not another to-do app.**

The after version is 6 words, names the product, differentiates from competitors, and reads as written by a person, not marketing.

## What to NOT do

- Don't put the brand name in the caption (Apple shows the app name above the screenshot)
- Don't use sentence case if the brand voice is title case (EduSupervise uses sentence case throughout)
- Don't use emojis or special characters — they render inconsistently across App Store localizations
- Don't add @ mentions or hashtags (App Store removes them anyway)
- Don't repeat the app description text — screenshots need a different angle

## Localization note

These captions are English-only. For non-English markets, the App Store has separate caption fields per language. Don't machine-translate — the cultural adaptation matters (German schools don't use "duty", French schools say "surveillance", etc.). Plan a real translation pass for v1.1 if you go international.
